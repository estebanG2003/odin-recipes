German Potato Salad

Home

Description

German Potato Salad is a warm, comforting side dish made with tender potatoes tossed in a tangy bacon vinaigrette.
Unlike creamy versions, this style uses no mayo — just bold flavors from vinegar, mustard, onions,
and smoky bacon, perfect alongside sausages, schnitzel, or roast meats.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900 g) waxy potatoes (Yukon Gold or red potatoes work well)
  • 6 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup (60 ml) apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) water
  • 1 tbsp Dijon or German mustard
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • Optional: 1-2 tbsp olive oil or reserved bacon fat for extra richness

Steps

  1. Boil the Potatoes
    • Place whole potatoes (with skin) in a large pot of salted water.
    • Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until fork-tender.
    • Drain and let cool slightly, then peel and slice into 1/4-inch thick rounds.
  2. Cook the Bacon
    • In a skillet, cook chopped bacon until crispy.
    • Remove bacon and set aside on a paper towel-lined plate.
    • Leave 1-2 tbsp of bacon fat in the pan.
  3. Make the Warm Dressing
    • In the same pan with the bacon fat, sauté chopped onions over medium heat until soft (2-3 minutes).
    • Add vinegar, water, mustard, and sugar. Stir and simmer for 2-3 minutes.
    • Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Combine
    • Gently toss the warm sliced potatoes with the dressing and bacon in a large bowl.
    • Let the salad sit for 10-15 minutes to absorb the flavors.
    • Sprinkle with chopped parsley before serving.

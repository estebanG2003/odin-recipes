Tiramisu

Home

Description

Tiramisu is a luxurious Italian dessert made of coffee-soaked ladyfingers layered with a rich and airy mascarpone cream,
topped with a dusting of cocoa powder. It's a no-bake treat that's creamy, light, and deeply satisfying — perfect for impressing guests or indulging yourself.

Ingredients

    For the Coffee Soak:
    • 1 cup (240 ml) brewed espresso or strong coffee, cooled
    • 2 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional, e.g. Kahlúa or Marsala wine)
    • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
    For The Cream Filling:
    • 3 large egg yolks
    • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
    • 1 cup (240 ml) heavy cream, cold
    • 1 cup (225 g) mascarpone cheese, softened
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    For Assembly:
    • 200-250 g (about 24 pieces) ladyfinger biscuits (savoiardi)
    • Unsweetened cocoa powder (for dusting)
    • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Coffee Soak
    • Brew strong coffee or espresso and let it cool.
    • Stir in the liqueur (if using) and sugar. Set aside.
  2. Make the Cream Filling
    • In a heatproof bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar together.
    • Place over a pot of simmering water (double boiler) and whisk constantly for 5-7 minutes until pale and thick.
    • Remove from heat and let cool.
    • In a separate bowl, whip heavy cream to stiff peaks.
    • Gently fold mascarpone and vanilla into the yolk mixture, then fold in the whipped cream until smooth and fluffy.
  3. Assemble the Tiramisu
    • Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture (don’t soak too long).
    • Arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a dish.
    • Spread half of the mascarpone cream over the ladyfingers.
    • Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining cream.
    • Smooth the top.
  4. Chill and Serve
    • Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours (overnight is best).
    • Before serving, dust generously with cocoa powder and add chocolate shavings if desired.
